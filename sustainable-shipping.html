Sustainable Shipping Optimization

View on GitHub

The Challenge

Large retailers partner with multiple carriers across regions—but every vendor reports delivery time, cost, and emissions differently. Without a unified view, sustainability goals clashed with budget targets: how do you cut carbon impact without driving up expenses?

My Approach

  • Data Integration: Built robust ETL pipelines in SQL and Python to ingest and normalize multi-vendor shipping logs, capturing delivery times, per-mile costs, and emissions factors.
  • Feature Engineering: Created metrics for “cost per emission unit” and “average delay penalty,” enabling apples-to-apples comparisons across carriers.
  • Regression Modeling: Applied linear regression to quantify trade-offs—predicting emissions change per dollar of cost increase.
  • Scenario Simulation: Modeled a 3-day delivery alternative across top routes, projecting both cost and carbon outcomes.
  • Dashboarding: Designed a Tableau dashboard to let stakeholders filter by route, vendor, and service level, visualizing the emissions–cost frontier in real time.

Results & Impact

  • 28% Emissions Reduction at only 5.5% Additional Cost by shifting select routes to the optimized 3-day window.
  • Presented findings to logistics leadership, who incorporated the strategy into their sustainability roadmap—potentially avoiding thousands of tons of CO₂ annually.
  • The interactive dashboard remains in use for vendor negotiations, aligning environmental targets with procurement decisions.
